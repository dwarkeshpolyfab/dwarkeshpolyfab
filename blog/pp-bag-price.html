Calculation Method

PP Bag Price Calculation Formula

Use this industry-standard formula to calculate approximate PP bag cost. For precise pricing with current raw material rates, use our calculator.

Basic PP Bag Weight Formula

Bag Weight (grams) = (Length" × Width" × GSM) ÷ 1000 × 2

Material Cost = Bag Weight (kg) × PP Rate per kg

Final Price = Material Cost + Lamination + Printing + Liner + Conversion Charges

Example: 24x36" bag, 80 GSM, plain, no printing
Weight = (24 × 36 × 80) ÷ 1000 × 2 = 138.24 grams
If PP rate = ₹110/kg → Material cost = 0.138 kg × ₹110 = ₹15.18
Add conversion charges (₹2-₹3) = Final price: ₹17-₹18 per bag

What is Filler in PP Bags?

Filler, also called ash content or calcium carbonate (CaCO₃), is a white powder additive mixed with polypropylene during the extrusion process. It's used to reduce material costs since CaCO₃ is cheaper than virgin PP granules.

How Filler Affects PP Bag Properties

  • Strength: Higher filler reduces tensile strength and tear resistance. Bags with 20% filler are 30-40% weaker than virgin PP bags.
  • Flexibility: Filler makes fabric stiffer and less flexible, increasing risk of cracking at folds and seams.
  • Print Quality: High filler content reduces print adhesion and color vibrancy. BOPP printing requires low filler (0-5%).
  • Moisture Resistance: Filler is hydrophilic (absorbs water), reducing moisture barrier properties.
  • Weight: Filler is denser than PP, so bags with filler weigh more but aren't necessarily stronger.

Our Recommendation

For most applications, we recommend 5-10% filler as the optimal balance between cost and quality. For food-grade, export, or heavy-duty applications, use 0-5% filler (premium grade). Avoid bags with >15% filler unless cost is the only priority and product is non-critical.

PP bag prices range from ₹3.50 to ₹15+ per piece depending on size, GSM, lamination, and printing. Standard 24x36 inch plain PP bags (70 GSM) start at ₹3.50-₹5, while BOPP laminated printed bags (100+ GSM) cost ₹8-₹15 per piece for bulk orders.

GSM (Grams per Square Meter) directly impacts PP bag cost. Higher GSM means thicker, stronger fabric but higher price. Each 10 GSM increase adds approximately ₹0.50-₹1.00 per bag. Standard bags use 70-90 GSM (₹3.50-₹6), while heavy-duty bags use 100-140 GSM (₹7-₹15+).

Filler percentage (also called ash content) is calcium carbonate (CaCO3) added to PP fabric to reduce cost. Standard filler is 5-10%, economy grade uses 15-20%. Higher filler reduces price by 10-15% but decreases strength and flexibility. Premium bags use 0-5% filler for maximum strength.

Printing cost depends on number of colors: 1 color adds ₹0.50-₹1, 2-3 colors add ₹1-₹2.50, 4-6 colors add ₹2.50-₹5 per bag. Additionally, there's a one-time cylinder charge of ₹2,000-₹3,000 per color (reusable for future orders). BOPP printing costs more but provides superior quality.
