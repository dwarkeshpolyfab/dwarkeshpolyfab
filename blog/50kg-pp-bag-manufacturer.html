How to Choose the Right 50kg PP Bag Manufacturer

1. Confirm Actual Manufacturing Capability

Many suppliers are traders, not manufacturers. For 50kg PP woven bags, buying directly from a manufacturer gives better control over fabric GSM, lamination, stitching, printing, dispatch schedule and repeat-order consistency. Dwarkesh Polyfab is based in Morbi, Gujarat and supplies PP woven bags, BOPP laminated bags and industrial packaging across India.

2. Match Bag Size with Product Density

A 50kg rice bag, 50kg fertilizer bag and 50kg cement bag may not use the same size because the volume of each product is different. Lightweight bulky products need more bag volume, while dense powders and granules may need compact dimensions. For better filling and stacking, share the product name before finalizing bag size.

3. Select GSM Based on Handling Risk

GSM affects fabric strength, bag weight and price. A lower GSM may reduce price, but it can increase tearing, seam failure and complaints during transport. For heavy-duty 50kg packaging, choose GSM after considering product weight, loading method, storage duration, transport distance and stacking height.

4. Use Lamination or Liner for Moisture-Sensitive Products

Plain PP woven bags are economical for dry, non-sensitive materials. Laminated PP woven bags offer better moisture resistance and a smoother printing surface. For powders, hygroscopic products and export consignments, a PE inner liner can provide extra protection against moisture and leakage.

5. Choose Printing According to Market Position

If the bag is used only for warehouse dispatch, single-color printing may be enough. If the bag is used for retail, dealer network or export, multicolor printing or BOPP laminated printing improves product presentation and brand recall. Printing can include brand logo, net weight, grade, product instructions, batch details and handling icons.
