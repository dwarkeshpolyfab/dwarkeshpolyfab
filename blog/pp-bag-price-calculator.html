How is PP Bag Price Actually Calculated? The Formula Explained

Understanding the price formula helps you make smarter decisions when comparing supplier quotes or choosing between specifications. The price of a PP woven bag comes from four cost components:

PP Bag Price Formula
Bag Weight (g) = (Width + 2×Gusset) × Height × 2 × GSM ÷ 10,000

Fabric Cost = Bag Weight (kg) × PP Granual Rate (₹/kg)
Lamination Cost = Bag Area (m²) × BOPP Rate (₹/m²) [if applicable]
Printing Cost = ₹ per bag based on colours and coverage

Final Bag Price = Fabric Cost + Lamination + Printing
Note: PP resin rate fluctuates with crude oil prices. Our calculator updates rates regularly to reflect current Morbi market prices. Individual supplier margins and order size also affect final pricing.

The single biggest driver of bag cost is GSM combined with bag size. A larger bag at higher GSM uses significantly more raw material than a smaller bag at lower GSM — and raw material is 60–75% of total bag cost. This is why comparing bags purely on "price per bag" without knowing the GSM and size is misleading — you may be comparing a 70 GSM bag with an 100 GSM bag that is completely different in strength and material content.
