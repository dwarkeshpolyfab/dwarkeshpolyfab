The winning bag is not the cheapest sample on the table. It is the cheapest bag that survives your filling, stacking and delivery route. That is why low GSM, tape quality, denier, weaving, bag size and stitching must be selected together.

About "2 GSM" language: Some buyers use local terms like "2 GSM bag", "silver 2.5 gram" or "gold 2.5 gram." For final production, confirm the actual fabric weight, finished bag weight, lamination, BOPP layer, stitching and sample performance.

Packaging Tips

1

Test with your real material

Salt, sand and dolomite powder do not behave the same. Test filling, dropping, stacking and loading before bulk order.

2

Do not buy only by GSM

Low GSM can work, but tape quality, weave, stitching and bag size decide whether the bag survives the route.

3

Use BOPP where buyers see the bag

For salt brands, one side BOPP gives a cleaner front face without making the full bag too costly.

4

Share monthly quantity first

High-volume buyers get better guidance when production volume, delivery city and material type are clear.

Smart FAQs

What is a one time use 25kg PP bag?

It is a low-cost PP woven bag made for one dispatch cycle. It is useful when a buyer needs economical 25kg packing for high-volume material.

Which industries use use and throw bags?

Salt, sand, dolomite powder, minerals, lime powder and construction material industries commonly use them for bulk dispatch.

Which size is best for dolomite powder?

19x34 and 19x32 are common for 25kg dolomite powder. Final size depends on powder density and filling method.

Which size is best for salt?

19x33 and 19x32 are common for salt packing. One side BOPP is useful when front printing and presentation matter.

Can low GSM bags safely carry 25kg?

Yes, when fabric quality, tape strength, weave, stitching and bag size are selected correctly. Sample testing is important.

What is silver or gold 2.5 gram material?

These are local buyer terms for lightweight material options. Confirm actual fabric, lamination, BOPP layer and sample before order.
