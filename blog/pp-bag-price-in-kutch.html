What Changes PP Bag Price?

Use this simple price logic before comparing suppliers. The lowest quote is useful only when the bag still fits your material and delivery route.

01

GSM and fabric quality

Higher GSM, better tape quality and better weaving increase weight and strength. Economy quality can work for short-cycle packing, but it must be tested.

02

Width and length

Large bags consume more fabric. Correct size also saves cost because loose bags waste material and tight bags create stitching failures.

03

Lamination and liner

Lamination improves outer protection and print finish. Liner adds inner protection for fine powder, salt, chemical and moisture-sensitive products.

04

Printing and BOPP

Flexo printing is practical for basic branding. BOPP gives a premium retail look but requires artwork, film, lamination and higher planning.

05

Stitching and finish

Single stitch, double stitch, hemmed top, heat cut, bottom fold and liner stitch affect bag handling, leakage control and final cost.

06

Quantity and dispatch

MOQ, monthly volume and delivery location around Gandhidham, Mundra, Kandla, Bhuj or Anjar affect commercial pricing and production planning.

What is the PP bag price in Kutch?

PP bag price in Kutch changes by size, GSM, fabric quality, lamination, printing, liner, stitching, quantity and dispatch location. Share specs for final pricing.

Which GSM is best for 50kg PP bags?

There is no single best GSM for every 50kg bag. The correct selection depends on material density, filling method, stacking height and delivery route.

Are laminated PP bags better?

Laminated PP bags are better when you need improved finish, basic moisture support, cleaner printing and better handling appearance.

When do I need a liner?

Use liner for powder, salt, fertilizer, chemicals, food grains or moisture-sensitive products where inner protection or leakage control is important.

Can I print my brand on PP bags?

Yes. You can choose plain, one-side print, two-side print or BOPP printed bags depending on your branding requirement and budget.
