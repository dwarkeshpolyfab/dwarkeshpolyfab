Complete Guide to Choosing the Right Bags for Rice Packaging

A practical guide for rice mill owners, traders and exporters who want to choose the best bag for their rice brand and target market.

Why Bag Choice Matters for Rice Packaging

The packaging bag is the first thing a rice buyer sees before buying. In wholesale markets, a plain strong PP bag does the job. But in retail stores, supermarkets and online platforms — the bag is your brand. A poor quality or dull looking rice bag can push buyers toward competitors even if your rice quality is better.

Choosing the right bag also protects your rice from moisture, pests and breakage during transport and storage — which directly affects the quality your customer receives and your reputation as a supplier.

Choosing the Right Size

Rice bags come in multiple sizes and choosing the right one depends on your target buyer — retail consumer, wholesale trader or export buyer.

  • 1 KG and 2 KG: Online retail, gifting, premium basmati brands
  • 5 KG: Supermarket retail, household consumers
  • 10 KG: Mid-range retail, small families and restaurants
  • 25 KG: Most common — wholesale traders, hotels, caterers
  • 50 KG: Bulk wholesale, rice mills to distributors

Which GSM is Right for Rice Bags?

For rice packaging the recommended GSM range is 75 to 100 GSM depending on the bag size and handling conditions.

  • 5 KG and 10 KG bags: 70 to 80 GSM is sufficient for lighter loads
  • 25 KG bags: 80 to 90 GSM gives good strength for standard handling
  • 50 KG bags: 90 to 110 GSM is recommended for heavy load bags
Important: Always use food grade virgin polypropylene for rice bags. Bags made from recycled PP or mixed material can transfer odor and are not safe for food contact. At Dwarkesh Polyfab all rice bags are made from 100% virgin food grade polypropylene.

Should You Use Inner PE Liner?

An inner PE (polyethylene) liner is a thin plastic bag placed inside the woven bag before filling. It creates a sealed inner barrier that prevents moisture entry from all sides. For rice being stored in humid areas, transported during monsoon season or exported by sea — inner liner is strongly recommended. It adds a small cost per bag but significantly reduces spoilage and moisture damage complaints.

Printing Tips for Rice Brand Bags

  • Include your brand name prominently in large font for shelf visibility
  • Mention rice variety clearly — Basmati, Sona Masoori, PR-14 etc.
  • Print net weight and FSSAI license number as required by law
  • Add your phone number or WhatsApp for repeat buyer contact
  • Use appetizing food photography or grain imagery to attract retail buyers
  • For export bags — add country of origin and importer details
